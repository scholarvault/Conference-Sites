## 2026-05-02 - Mass-Generated Conference Clustering
**Source:** World Research Library (WRL)
**Pattern Found:** Multiple uniquely branded conferences (e.g., ISER, IRAJ, Research World, Researchfora) scheduled on the exact same dates in the exact same cities (e.g., April 29 in Kuala Lumpur). URL image paths often share identical prefixes like `PROCE-IRAJ-`.
**Reliability:** High confidence that this is a mass-generation/mega-conference network pattern.
**Watch For:** Overlapping dates and locations across supposedly different conference organizers listed on aggregator sites. Cross-promotion links in "Associates" or "Partners" footers connecting these libraries and alert sites.

## 2026-05-02 - Buzzword Salad Titles and SDG Hijacking
**Source:** Academic Research Library
**Pattern Found:** Conference proceeding titles that string together an excessive number of broad, unrelated trending topics (e.g., "Human Wellbeing, Sustainable Development, and Intelligent Innovation in a Changing World"). Strong overt reliance on matching UN SDG categories to appear legitimate.
**Reliability:** Medium. Legitimate conferences also use SDG keywords, but the combination of *many* unrelated broad terms is a signal.
**Watch For:** Titles attempting to cover too many disciplines simultaneously, especially clustering around late April / early May dates.
